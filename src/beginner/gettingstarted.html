Getting Started

Goals

Setting clear, attainable goals is a crucial step in your fitness journey, especially when starting out in the gym. It's easy to feel overwhelmed by the array of machines, exercises, and potential workout routines. By identifying specific, achievable objectives, you can focus your efforts and measure your progress, which in turn, helps maintain motivation and reduces feelings of being overwhelmed. Goals give you direction and purpose each time you step into the gym, ensuring your workout routine is structured and aligned with what you want to achieve.

Start small to build confidence and momentum. For example, if your ultimate goal is to run a 5K, begin by aiming to run for a continuous 5 minutes, gradually increasing your time each week. This approach keeps the process manageable and less intimidating. Remember, consistency over perfection is key. Regularly showing up and sticking to your goals, even on days when progress seems slow, lays the foundation for long-term success.

Sets and Reps

In the context of physical exercise, "reps" and "sets" are fundamental concepts used to structure and describe workout routines. "Reps," short for repetitions, refer to the number of times you perform a specific exercise without stopping. For example, doing ten push-ups in a row counts as ten reps. On the other hand, "sets" are groups of repetitions performed sequentially, separated by rest periods. If you complete three rounds of ten push-ups, taking a break between each round, you have done three sets of ten reps each. This method of organizing workouts helps in managing the intensity, volume, and focus of your training sessions, allowing for targeted muscle engagement, endurance building, and strength development. By adjusting the number of reps and sets, individuals can tailor their workout programs to meet specific fitness goals, whether they're aiming for muscle gain, weight loss, or overall health improvement.

Core

How to engage your core

  1. Start with Posture: Stand or sit up tall with your feet hip-width apart. Imagine a string pulling the top of your head towards the ceiling, lengthening your spine.
  2. Breathe Deeply: Focus on breathing deeply into your diaphragm (the area just below your ribcage). Inhale deeply through your nose, allowing your chest and lower belly to rise.
  3. Tighten the Muscles: As you exhale, gently tighten the muscles around your stomach and hips, as if you're bracing for an impact. Imagine you’re trying to zip up a tight pair of jeans or drawing your belly button towards your spine.
  4. Maintain Breathing: Keep the muscles engaged while maintaining your ability to breathe. It's crucial not to hold your breath—continue breathing deeply and steadily.
  5. Practice Holding: Start by holding this engaged position for 10-15 seconds, gradually increasing the duration as your endurance improves.
