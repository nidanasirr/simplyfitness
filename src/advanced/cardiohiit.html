Cardio (HIIT)

High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT) is a form of cardiovascular exercise that alternates between short bursts of intense activity and periods of lower-intensity recovery or rest. This training method is known for its efficiency in burning calories, improving cardiovascular fitness, and increasing metabolism. HIIT workouts typically last anywhere from 10 to 30 minutes, making them ideal for individuals with busy schedules.

The effectiveness of HIIT lies in its ability to elevate the heart rate quickly and maintain it at a high level throughout the workout. This leads to a phenomenon known as excess post-exercise oxygen consumption (EPOC), or the afterburn effect, where the body continues to burn calories at an elevated rate even after the workout has ended.
